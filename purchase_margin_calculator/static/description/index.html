Purchase Margin Calculator

Track last purchase cost, UoM conversion, and real margin on products

Odoo Version: 17.0

Key Features

Last Purchase Cost Tracking

Automatically captures the last purchase price, vendor, date, and order reference when a PO is confirmed.

UoM Conversion

Converts purchase cost to sale UoM automatically. Buy in kg, sell in units — the margin is calculated correctly.

Real Margin Calculation

Computes margin using the effective sale price (POS pricelist or list price) vs. the converted purchase cost.

Price Alerts

Visual badges (OK, Warning, Critical) based on a configurable margin factor to quickly spot underpriced products.

Purchase Price History

Keeps the last 10 purchase prices per product with vendor, date, UoM, and converted price for trend analysis.

Suggested Price

Calculates a suggested minimum sale price based on the margin factor (e.g., cost x 1.30 for 30% margin).

Price Alert Levels

OK Warning Critical No Data
  • OK: Sale price meets or exceeds the suggested price (cost x factor)
  • Warning: Sale price is above cost +10% but below the suggested price
  • Critical: Sale price is below cost +10% — margin is dangerously low
  • No Data: No purchase history or no sale price configured

How It Works

  • On PO confirmation, the module captures the purchase price and records it in the history.
  • A daily cron job recalculates costs for all purchasable products.
  • The margin factor is configurable in Purchase Settings (default: 1.30 = 30% margin).
  • New "Purchase Cost & Margin" tab on product form shows all data at a glance.
  • Optional columns on product list view: Last Cost, Suggested Price, Margin %, Alert.
  • Search filters for Critical, Warning, OK, and No Data products.
  • Does NOT overwrite standard_price — safe to use alongside standard costing methods.

Configuration

  • Go to Purchase > Settings
  • Find the Purchase Margin Calculator section
  • Set the Target Margin Factor (default 1.30)
  • The module auto-populates history from existing confirmed POs on install
